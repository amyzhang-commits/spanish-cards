📚 Spanish Tenses & Moods Reference

Learn about Spanish conjugation patterns before generating your flashcards

📊 Indicative Mood

States facts, describes reality

Present Indicative

Describes actions happening now or habitual actions

Example: "Yo hablo español" (I speak Spanish)

Preterite

Completed actions in the past with specific time

Example: "Ayer hablé con María" (Yesterday I spoke with María)

Imperfect

Ongoing or habitual actions in the past

Example: "Cuando era niño, hablaba mucho" (When I was a child, I used to talk a lot)

Future

Actions that will happen in the future

Example: "Mañana hablaré con el jefe" (Tomorrow I will speak with the boss)

Present Perfect

Actions completed recently or with present relevance

Example: "He hablado con ella hoy" (I have spoken with her today)

Past Perfect

Actions completed before another past action

Example: "Ya había hablado cuando llegaste" (I had already spoken when you arrived)

Future Perfect

Actions that will be completed before a future point

Example: "Para entonces habré hablado con todos" (By then I will have spoken with everyone)

🤔 Subjunctive Mood

Expresses doubt, emotion, desire, or hypothetical situations

Present Subjunctive

Used with present-time expressions of doubt or emotion

Example: "Espero que hables español" (I hope you speak Spanish)

Imperfect Subjunctive

Used with past-time expressions of doubt or emotion

Example: "Si hablara español..." (If I spoke Spanish...)

Future Subjunctive

Rarely used, found in legal texts and proverbs

Example: "Si hablare español..." (If I were to speak Spanish...)

Present Perfect Subjunctive

Completed actions in contexts requiring subjunctive

Example: "Dudo que haya hablado" (I doubt that he has spoken)

Past Perfect Subjunctive

Past completed actions in hypothetical contexts

Example: "Si hubiera hablado..." (If I had spoken...)

💭 Conditional Mood

Expresses hypothetical or polite situations

Simple Conditional

What would happen under certain conditions

Example: "Hablaría español si pudiera" (I would speak Spanish if I could)

Perfect Conditional

What would have happened in the past

Example: "Habría hablado antes" (I would have spoken earlier)

⚡ Imperative Mood

Gives commands, requests, or instructions

Affirmative Imperative

Positive commands and requests

Example: "¡Habla más despacio!" (Speak more slowly!)

Negative Imperative

Negative commands using subjunctive forms

Example: "¡No hables tan rápido!" (Don't speak so fast!)
